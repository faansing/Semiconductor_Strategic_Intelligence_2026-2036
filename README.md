## **5 Key Takeaways**

1. Durable money in semis is limited to a few spots — mainly TSMC, ASML, KLA, and the design tool guys. Everything else is either cyclical or under pressure from customers building alternatives.

2. A lot of the high-quality names are already priced pretty full. You own them for steady compounding, not because they're cheap or about to re-rate higher.

3. Memory (HBM) is at peak earnings right now. The low-looking multiples are a trap — treat it as a trade you rent for the up-cycle and get out before the reversion hits.

4. NVIDIA is a real platform with serious economics today, but it's contestable. Size it as a bet on how well margins hold up against custom silicon and open alternatives, not as a forever core holding.

5. The biggest risk over the next decade isn't a sudden crash — it's a long, slow monetization grind where AI revenue grows but returns disappoint, multiples compress, and there's no clear exit signal. Build the book around the insulated compounders and keep tight risk controls.
